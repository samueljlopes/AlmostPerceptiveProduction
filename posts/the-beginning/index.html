Everything must have a beginning.

Jun 30 2020

“There are things known and there are things unknown, and in between are the doors of perception.‚Äù - Aldous Huxley.”#

It’s always difficult to begin something, I find. It’s very easy to declare to yourself, ‘I’m going to do that thing that I’ve always wanted to do, and I’m going to start… wait for it… now!’ You might get a warm glow for a little while, flush with your own decisiveness and go-getter attitude, but in the heat of the moment, you never actually get around to doing anything at all. ‘Oh, well’, you might think, ‘there’s always tomorrow.’ And there usually is, short of some horrific accident.

However, the ability to turn that yearning into action is one that must be flexed, over time, like a muscle. This first post is a gentle warmup to what I hope will turn out to be a rather excellent mental work-out.

Get to the point, you great nit.#

What you such read through, dear reader, was my perception of something. You may think differently, (ie. you are an amazing person who has followed through on every self-commitment you have ever made) and that is utterly and completely fine. That is why I have titled this little experiment ‘Almost Perceptive’. To be perceptive is to possess a key to a locked door that everyone knows can open, but nobody has actually put in the effort to call a decent locksmith. There I go again. Having another perception of something.

It’s very arrogant of me to assume that my thoughts and perceptions are of any use to anyone, although I hope they are. This whole affair is quite pointless otherwise.

So, if all goes to plan, there are many other posts besides this one. And that if I’ve not very perceptive at the moment, please know that I’m trying my utmost to be almost perceptive some time in the near future.
